Black Hole Discovery

Astronomers have detected a black hole displaced nearly a kilosparsec from the center of a dwarf galaxy 230 million light-years away, actively feeding and producing radio jets.

The James Webb Space Telescope:

This revolutionary telescope is allowing scientists to study the universe in unprecedented detail,from the formation of galaxies to the atmospheres of exoplanets.

Exoplanet Discoveries:

Thousands of exoplanets have been discovered, and some are believed to be located in the habitable zones of their stars, where life could potentially thrive.

Did You Know?

  1. The Universe is still expanding:

    2. Scientists have observed that the Universe is still growing, with galaxies moving away from each other.

  2. There are more stars in the universe than grains of sand:

    3. The number of stars in the observable Universe is estimated to be around 100 billion trillion.

  3. Venus rotates in the opposite direction:

    4. Unlike most planets, Venus rotates from east to west, a phenomenon known as retrograde rotation.
